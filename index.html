Worldwide Renewal Energy Produced From 2000 - 2021 (KTOE)

The map represents the amount of renewal energy produced by different countries from the year 2000 to 2021. The countries are shaded in varying colors, with dark green indicating the highest renewable energy production and light blue indicating the lowest. Notably, China, Brazil, India & the US are shaded in dark green, signifying that they have the highest renewable energy production. The amount of energy generated in KTOE (Kilo Tons of Oil Equivalent), a unit of energy.

Energy Produced per Source (TWH)

Grams of CO2 Emitted per KWH

Each country has different dominant ways of producing energy. As evident from the stacked bar, one of the most famous Source for energy generation is Coal. However, when we cross compare that with the chart on the right, it is evident that Coal is one of the least eco friendly ways of generating energy. The amount of CO2 produced is more that double of the other non renewable sources. Thus, the shift towards renewable energy is greatly beneficial.

Worldwide Energy Consumption per Capita From 2000 - 2021 (KWH/Person)

The map represents the amount of energy used per captia from the year 2000 to 2021. The countries are shaded in varying colors, with dark red indicating the highest renewable energy production and light orange indicating the lowest. Notably, Canada, the US, Australia, UAE, Oman & Saudi Arabia are shaded in dark red, signifying that they have the highest energy Consumption. The amount of energy consumed per capita is in KWH/Person, which represents the energy used by each person in Kilo Watt Hours

Energy Consumption Per Category in Australia from 2017 - 2021 (KWH)

There are seven main categories: Agriculture, Commercial, Construction, Mining, Households, Manufacturing, and Services. On average, most of the energy used in Australia goes towards Households, with manufacturing coming in second. This is expected, as there are a number of houses in Australia and each one of those houses uses energy for a number of reasons. We can also observe that the category that uses the lowest amount of energy is agriculture, which is expected, as generally speaking, agriculture does not require enormous amounts of energy.
